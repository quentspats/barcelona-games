How you actually meet people

The mechanism, not the games.

The games are an excuse. This is what turns 30 strangers into a group chat.

  1. 01

    You arrive and get a team colour.

    Wristband by colour, name on the list, flag on the sand. 90 seconds at the table.

  2. 02

    You walk to your flag.

    Big colored fabric on a pole, four or five teammates already there. No one is looking for you in a crowd.

  3. 03

    First job: learn five names. Invent a chant.

    Ten minutes. The chant is mandatory. The chant is also always terrible. That's how you bond.

  4. 04

    Every game forces hand-offs and names.

    Quick rotations, lots of cheering. Standing around isn't an option.

  5. 05

    Tapas becomes a group chat.

    Snacks, drinks, chants, awards. By the time you walk out of the bar, the WhatsApp group is already buzzing.

The games

Ten games on the table. Some on the day.

Here are the ten challenges we're cooking up for the first public edition. The exact line-up and any surprise twists are revealed on the day. Expect a mix of teamwork, speed, strategy, coordination, agility and trust.

  • Coordination

    Water Balloon Volleyball

    Four-person launch crew using a shared fabric sling to absorb, aim and return a fragile water balloon without bursting it.

  • Teamwork

    Tunnel Ball

    Full-line relay where players stay low and move the ball seamlessly through the human tunnel. Rhythm, awareness, fast collective transitions.

  • Agility

    Clothes Peg Relay

    Players race to transfer pegs using only their mouths. Balance, composure and relay timing. As tactical as it is hilarious.

  • Trust

    Pass the Cup (Balloon Edition)

    By controlling airflow through balloons, teammates suspend, receive and redirect a floating cup. An unexpectedly technical test of trust and coordination.

  • Coordination

    Back-to-Back Ball Bounce

    Three players move as one unit, keeping back contact and ball control at the same time. A simple dribble becomes a choreography of timing and communication.

  • Teamwork

    Human Carry Obstacle Course

    Teams transport one teammate through a sandy obstacle route. Speed, safety and stability while adapting together under pressure.

  • Communication

    Cone-to-Cone Catch Relay

    Each clean throw advances the line. Teams combine accurate passing, quick rotations and smooth communication to keep the sequence alive.

  • Strategy

    Seated Beach Tug-of-War

    Seated low in the sand, teams synchronize posture, grip and timing. Raw pulling power becomes a coordinated endurance battle.

  • Trust

    Blindfold Beach Maze

    One player navigates a cone course without sight, relying entirely on teammates' directions. Trust, clarity and calm leadership decide who finishes first.

  • Speed

    Bucket Brigade Splash Relay

    Teams shuttle water down the line from cup to cup. Small mistakes compound fast. Precision, rhythm and strategy decide the winners.
