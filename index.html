Your Saturday Plan

Hour by hour. Whistle to last drink.

Arrive at 15:00. Last whistle at 19:00. Then we walk together to a beach bar.

  1. 15:00

    Arrival at the welcome tent

    Step onto the sand at Platja del Fòrum and look for our welcome tent next to the volleyball net — five colored flags wave above it, impossible to miss. Come alone? Most people do. The whole point is connection, so say hi to whoever's nearest, they're as new here as you are. We'll greet you by name, hand you your bandana, and walk you over to your team. First mission with your four new teammates: cook up a chant (and a dance if you're feeling brave) to introduce yourselves to the rest of the crew.

  2. 15:30

    Opening ceremony

    Quick welcome and format walkthrough. Your team gets 15 minutes with the new crew to prep, then each team gets one minute to present their chant. Loud, fast, no overthinking.

  3. 16:00 – 18:30

    The games

    And now for the best part of the day. A rotating selection of surprise games designed to cut through language barriers, get you moving, meet plenty of new people, and have a stupid amount of fun (while sneaking in the workout you'd have skipped Saturday morning). Fast rotations. Points tracked. One big finale.

  4. 19:00

    Walk to the beach bar

    Closing ceremony on the sand: medals, funny awards, group photo. Then we walk to a chiringuito for pica-pica (on us) and drinks (your call). Stay as long as the vibe holds.

The Social Sports Games

A mix of games. A lot of surprises.

Beach challenges, team relays, trust games, water games, balance tasks, coordination tests, speed runs. A rotating selection you'll never see twice. Different every edition. Loud every edition.

✦Sample challenges. The actual line-up stays a surprise.

  • Guide your teammate through the course using only communication.

  • Move water down the line with speed, rhythm, and teamwork.

  • Pass, catch, rotate, and keep the sequence alive.

  • Move as one team through a sandy obstacle route.

  • Strength matters, but timing and coordination win.

  • Launch, catch, and return without bursting the balloon.

  • A fast team relay where timing and transitions decide everything.

  • Transfer the pegs across the line using only your mouth.

  • Pass the cup down the line without dropping or popping.

What to bring

Swimsuit, water bottle (we'll have a few spares per team), and your social + sports skills. We handle the rest.

Every game is designed to make you connect, compete, and laugh with your team (and a little with the rival teams too — no promises about who you'll text first on Sunday).

The bandana system

Bandana. Flag. Team. Chant. Loud.

5 colors. 5 Barcelona landmarks. 5 teams of 6. We mix everyone up beforehand to balance nationalities, energy and accents (so no team accidentally ends up being five French people speaking only French). Every team is a healthy chaos of new faces. You grab your bandana, walk to your flag, and four teammates are already there waiting.
